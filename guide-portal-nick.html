The Work

Small wins, every session.

Agility isn't about cones for the sake of cones. It's first-step quickness, change of direction, balance under pressure — the things that decide 1v1 moments in a real game. Every session your child leaves with something they can feel: a sharper cut, a faster first step, a move that worked.

Kids don't fall in love with drills. They fall in love with getting better. My job is to make sure they notice it happening.

1

Meet & move

First session is light — we play, I watch how your child moves, and they get comfortable with me. Parents welcome pitchside.

2

Build the base

Footwork patterns, acceleration mechanics, and body control — built up in games and challenges, not lectures.

3

Take it to the game

Everything we train shows up on match day. I'll tell you what to watch for so you can see the progress yourself.
