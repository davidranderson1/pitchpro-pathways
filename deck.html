Option B · What We'd Build

His Website & Customer Experience

Features we can add to Sandro's website and back end — each one removes admin from Sandro's day and upgrades what a client feels.

  • Online booking + payments — clients pick a DARI assessment slot, pay or leave a deposit, and get automatic confirmations; no phone tag, no no-shows without a card on file.
  • Dynamics CRM client hub — every client, assessment, report, and conversation in one record; follow-up pipelines fire automatically (post-assessment check-in, reassessment due, dormant client win-back).
  • Digital intake & consent — forms filled before arrival, including parent/guardian consent for minors, flowing straight into the CRM — nothing re-typed, nothing on paper.
  • Client results portal — a login where clients see their Motion Age and progress over time; the reassessment sells itself when the graph is right there.
  • Automated reminders & rebooking — session reminders, report-delivery notifications, and reassessment nudges at the right intervals — recurring revenue on autopilot.
  • Customer service workflows — one shared inbox with response standards, post-session feedback surveys, and reviews routed to the site — the service experience becomes a sales asset.
  • Referral bridge to PitchPro Pathways — screenings booked through the platform land in the same CRM, so both businesses see one clean picture of every athlete.
Insights

Where the Leverage Is

  • The screening is the funnel. A $249 science-backed assessment is the lowest-friction first purchase a parent can make — and its report naturally recommends the $799–$1,499 programs. Every screening is a warm lead.
  • "Motion Age" is the hook. No youth soccer platform leads with clinical-grade movement data. One parent-friendly number makes the science sellable.
  • Reassessment = recurring revenue. Measuring progress means every athlete comes back — retention is built into the product.
  • One capability, two markets. Youth athletes and corporate executives buy the same assessment and the same guides — double revenue on the same fixed capacity.
  • Sandro's three roles need three answers. Service delivery, council credibility, and methodology ownership should each have their own terms — one blended handshake will get messy later.
  • The draft contains placeholder claims. "500+ pros developed", "98% parent satisfaction", the testimonials, and the guide roster beyond Sandro/Nick/Grego are illustrative — they must be replaced or removed before anything goes public.
